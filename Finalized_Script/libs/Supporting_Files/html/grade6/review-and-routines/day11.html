Establishing Routines (3 Min.)

Smooth Transitions

If students sit at tables or in groups, you may want to call one table at a time to transition to the whole-group meeting area by using a signal, such as a hand bell, clapping, or flashing the lights. Remind students to push the chair in quietly and walk silently to the meeting area.

Getting Ready for Whole-Group Instruction: Using Questioning to Understand Text

Review the T-chart from yesterday’s class. Ask students to volunteer the difference between thick and thin questions with examples of each. Refer to the Thick and Thin chart if students need prompting.

Ask students to turn and talk to discuss the differences between thick and thin questions and to be prepared to discuss their thoughts.




Objectives

Students will:

  • Transition efficiently between mini-lessons.
  • Practice active listening.

Additional Materials

  • Preselected book to read aloud
  • Thick and Thin Questions Chart

Support Tips

Reading, writing, and word study assessments may be administered throughout the week while students are working independently.

Read-Aloud and Book Discussion (17 Min.)

Read-Aloud: Using Questioning to Understand Text

Read any book from the suggested read-aloud list, or any other available book that is of interest to you. It may be helpful to preview the book in advance, writing down your questions on self-stick notes so that you can easily recall them during the read-aloud.

Ask: What do we know about asking questions?

Refer to Thick and Thin chart as needed.

Ask: How do you think asking questions helps a reader? How do readers figure out the answers to their questions? As I read aloud today, I am going to show you how I ask questions while reading.

Model the questions that come to mind as you read aloud to the class.

Ask students to turn and talk to a partner about the types of questions that the teacher asked.


Independent Reading (20 Min.)

Independent Reading: Questioning to Understand Text

Say: Now it is your turn to practice asking questions before, during, and after you read a book. Today, we are going to pick a shorter text to read as we practice asking questions. I have a variety of books to choose from in this basket. You will need to choose a book and get some self-stick notes and a pen or pencil before you sit down to read. As you preview your book, write down any questions you have. Then, begin reading and continue to write down questions as you read. You can post your self-stick notes in the book for now. We will use them again later today.

Allow students the opportunity to choose a picture book from your collection, get their self-stick notes, and get a pen or pencil before settling down to read. Walk around and confer with students as they read and ask questions.




Objectives

Students will:

  • Learn about asking questions while reading.
  • Practice asking questions while reading.

Additional Materials

  • Thick and Thin Questions Chart
  • Basket of books
  • Self-Stick notes

Writing to Sources (20 Min.)

A Response to Reading

Using the established signal, call students to the meeting area. Ask students to leave their independent reading books at their desks.

Say: We just finished practicing the strategy of asking questions while reading. How did it go?

Say: I want us to take a closer look at the questions I asked when I read earlier today. I am going to carefully pull all of the self-stick notes out of the book and stick them on a clean sheet of paper. Now, let’s go through and put an “A” for answered next to each question that was answered as I read the book. We need to note where we located the answers.

Read each question aloud, asking students to share whether the question was answered while reading the book or not. Model putting an “A” on self-stick notes that had an answer.

Say: I want to think about which of my questions helped me understand the book better.

Pause while reading through your questions again.

Say: I think this question, _________________________, was very helpful to me.

Explain to the students why this question helped you develop a deeper understanding of the book.

Say: Let’s practice. When I tell you to, you will go back and carefully pull your self-stick notes from your books and stick them on a sheet of paper. Then read through your questions and mark an “A” on any self-stick that has a question that was answered while you read your book. Then choose one of the questions that helped you understand the book more deeply. Write that question at the bottom of your page and explain why this question is key to your understanding of the book.




Objectives

Students will:

  • Review and analyze questions asked while reading.

Additional Materials

  • Read-aloud book and model questions from earlier
  • Blank paper
  • Students’ independent reading books and self-stick notes from earlier

Independent Writing (35 Min.)

Establishing Workstation Routines

Have a Rotation chart with student assignments ready to share with the class.

Say: We are going to practice having a few different workstations going at the same time. We will use this workstation chart to help us know what station we are supposed to be working in.

Explain to the students how to read the chart and who will be going where first. Remind students of the expectations for each workstation, and call students to move to the appropriate station to get started.

Reading Workstation Listening Workstation
Computer Workstation Writing Workstation

Walk around and monitor students as they work at the different stations. Answer any questions students may have or redirect students that need additional support. After ten minutes or so, ask students to finish up what they are working on. Praise students for any positive behaviors you noticed during this block of time. Offer any suggestions for expectations you feel the class should focus on next time.

Say: Now we are going to clean up and switch to a new workstation.

Direct students to look at the workstation chart to see where they are going next. Remind students of the expectations for rotation between workstations.

Repeat the rotational process one more time so that students have practiced getting started, cleaning up, and rotating to a new station three different times.




Objectives

Students will:

  • Practice rotating among the workstations.

Additional Materials

  • Chart paper
  • Markers

Support Tips

Based on your students’ needs, word study components can be taught this week by being added to the shared writing section for example: irregular plural nouns (man, men; foot, feet), silent letters (/n/ gn, kn; /r/ wr; /m/ mb), possessives (singular and plural), prefixes (un-, re-, dis-), and words with suffixes (-ful, -less).
