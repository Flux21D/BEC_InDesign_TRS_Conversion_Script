10 Analyze Genre Features: Persuasive Speech (15–20 MIN.) RI.5.1, RI.5.3, R.I.5.5, W.5.10, SL.5.1a, SL.5.1b, SL.5.1c, SL.5.1d

Engage Thinking

Remind students that the “Voting Rights Act Address” was a speech in which President Lyndon Johnson tried to persuade Congress to help him pass a law that would guarantee that all states protect citizens’ voting rights. The speech contains the words that reflect his personal point of view. Explain that this lesson will focus on understanding features of a persuasive speech.

Ask: What do you recall about the speech we just read? What stood out for you?

Introduce Genre

Display and create Features of a Persuasive Speech Web, and discuss each genre feature, using examples from “President Lyndon Johnson’s Voting Rights Act Address” as needed. Explain the following points about persuasive speeches:

  • A persuasive speech is a speech that tries to convince listeners to believe or do something. It expresses a strong point of view about an idea or problem. It includes facts and examples to make a case and usually suggests a solution.
  • In a speech, the speaker has a specific audience in mind and interacts directly with the audience. Often the speaker wants to change the audience’s opinions.
  • Speeches may contain powerful words to influence the listeners.

Sample Features of a Persuasive Speech Web

Productive Engagement: Peer Group

Distribute a two-column chart. Ask groups to skim and scan “President Lyndon Johnson’s Voting Rights Act Address” to find examples of each genre feature you introduced. Each group should designate a discussion director whose responsibility is to ensure that all students have the chance to contribute ideas. Students should share the information they are learning from reading this speech and discuss why this act was being established at this time in history. Ask them to label the examples in their text and be prepared to present their findings.

Give students no more than seven to eight minutes. Monitor their conversations to identify students who struggle and students who are able to find examples in the text.

Share

Call on members of each group to share the examples the group found for each feature of a persuasive speech. Encourage members of the other groups to build on the ideas by presenting additional information. Model sentence frames that students can use to link their comments to those of the presenters. For example:

  • Our group found an example of the genre feature _____ . We found _____.
  • We found an example of a different genre feature. We found _____.

If groups struggled to find specific features of a persuasive speech in the address, model how you identified features using the sample chart provided.

Apply Genre Understanding

Ask students to write a short paragraph in which they identify at least two examples from “President Lyndon Johnson’s Voting Rights Act Address” of facts or reasons that would convince them to end the discrimination of African American voters.
