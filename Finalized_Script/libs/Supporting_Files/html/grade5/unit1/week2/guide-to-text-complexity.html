Guide to Text Complexity

Text complexity dimensions from CCSS are scored on the following scale:

1 Low 2 Middle Low 3 Middle High 4 High

Grades 4 – 5
Lexile ®: 740L – 1010L

Extended Read 1: “The Dred Scott Decision”


Informational Social Studies
Quantitative Lexile® 890L
Qualitative Analysis of Text Complexity
Purpose and Levels of Meaning 2
  • The explicit purpose of this selection is to inform readers of the landmark “Dred Scott” case and to explain the importance of the case in the end as a catalyst for change.
Structure 2
  • This explanatory text follows a chronological nonfiction narrative and relies on sequence of events and cause and effect text structures.
Language Conventionality and Clarity 2
  • The selection uses mostly simple sentence structures and supports a fair amount of the academic vocabulary with descriptions, examples, and other context clues.
Knowledge Demands 2
  • Basic U.S. History and U.S. Government and Citizenship will assist readers in fully understanding the text but is not essential.
