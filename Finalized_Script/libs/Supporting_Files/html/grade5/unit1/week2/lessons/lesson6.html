Student Objectives

I will be able to:

  • Quote accurately from a text to answer a question.
  • Draw an inference using text evidence.
  • Share my understanding in partner conversations and in writing.

Additional Materials

  • Context Clues chart
  • Print or online dictionary

Integrated ELD

Substantial Support

Review with students what context clues are and how they can be used to help readers understand the meaning of a new word. Tell them that context clues can be especially helpful in subjects such as social studies and science, where many words are domain-specific, or used only in a specific context.

Support students as they use context clues to define domain-specific words by using the Context Clues chart. Provide these sentence frames to help students define each word.

The word _____ appears in paragraph _____. Other words surrounding this word include _____ and _____.

A context clue for the word _____ is _____.

From this, I can define the word _____ as _____.

If students cannot find context clues for a chosen word, instruct them to use a dictionary.

After the word has been properly defined, have students write a new sentence using the word.

Moderate Support

Provide the sentence frames above and ask students the following questions.

What does the word _____ mean?

What context clues support this definition?

How does the dictionary define the word _____?

Does this differ from the context clues? If so, how?

Ask students to write out the answers to these questions. Then have them write a new sentence with each word.

Light Support

Provide the sentence frames and questions above. Challenge students to define domain-specific words on their own. Then have them write sentences that combine two or more of the domain-specific words. Offer assistance as needed.

6Use Context Clues to Define Domain-Specific Words(15– 20 min.) RI.5.1, RI.5.4, RF.5.4c, SL.5.1a, SL.5.1b, SL.5.1c, SL.5.1d, L5.4a, L5.4c

Engage Thinking

Tell students that when they read an informational text, they may encounter domain-specific words that relate to the topic. Understanding these words helps readers to better read and comprehend the text. Explain that this lesson will focus on using context clues to read and define some of the words in “The Dred Scott Decision.”

Ask: How can using context clues support your reading?

Model

Remind students that domain-specific words are vocabulary terms used in a field, such as social studies or science. Explain that context clues are the words around a word that can help them read a word and understand its meaning. Context clues include synonyms, direct definition, description, and examples. Model how to analyze the context clues around the word secession in paragraph 3.

Sample modeling: In sentence 3, I see the word secession and circle it, because I’m not really sure how to read it or what it means. I will look at the words around this word to see if I can find clues. After the word I see a dash. The next part of the sentence says that they [Southern leaders] were threatening “splitting off the slave states from the Union.” I will underline this clue.

Sample modeling: Now, I will analyze the clue to help me understand the meaning of the word secession. The word splitting helps me to know that secession refers to separating, or breaking away, from something. The other words in the sentence indicate that the Southern leaders wanted the slave states to break away from the Union. Therefore, secession must mean “to break away from the Union.” I’m pretty sure this is an accurate definition, but I’ll reread the sentence to see if it makes more sense to me, and it does. I can also check the meaning in a dictionary if I need to.

Guided Practice

Ask students to skim and scan “The Dred Scott Decision” and circle any domain-specific words that they did not understand during the first reading, or that they think are especially important to understanding this informational text. Make a class list of the words and read them aloud. Distribute a Context Clues chart and have partners work together to underline the context clues that help them understand the words, define the word, and then reread to check their understanding. Remind them to look for synonyms, direct definitions, descriptions, and examples.

Students should then consult a dictionary and revise or expand their definitions as needed.

Reflect on the Strategy

Ask students to reflect on the strategy and its relevance to other content-area reading experiences by asking each set of partners to turn to another set of partners and briefly discuss a question: Why is knowing how to analyze context clues helpful?

Call on volunteers to give their answer. Reinforce students’ understanding that finding and analyzing context clues are strategies that will help them read fluently and understand a variety of texts.

Reread and Write to Apply Understanding

Ask students to write sentences using one or more of the words they defined. The sentences should demonstrate their understanding of the word’s meaning.
