9Introduce Long Vowels (15–20 min.) RF.5.3a, SL.5.1a, SL.5.1b, SL.5.1c, SL.5.1d

Introduce/Model

Reread paragraph 1 of “The Dred Scott Decision” and underline the words nation, freedom, and rights. Circle the letters a in nation, ee in freedom, and igh in rights and say the words aloud. Explain that each of these words has a long vowel sound and that long vowels can be spelled in many ways. In many words, the long vowel sound is found at the end of an open syllable, as in nation. In other words, a vowel pair such as ee stands for the long vowel sound. The letters igh stand for the long i sound. Sometimes, the spelling pattern CVCe signals a long vowel sound, as in life. Students will learn to recognize the letters and letter combinations that stand for long vowel sounds.

Display the words legal, later, growing, wife, and issue. Circle the letters that stand for the long vowel sound in each word. Point out that all of these words contain long vowel sounds.

Guided Practice

Display a five-column Word-Sort chart and a list of words to sort: be, states, leaders, over, refused, time, whole. Read each word aloud. Have students repeat the word after you and identify the column in which the word belongs. Remind students that the long vowel sounds can be spelled in a variety of ways.

Productive Engagement: Peer Group

Distribute blank five-column charts. Ask peer groups to work together to sort words into the appropriate columns on the chart as you read a list of additional words with a variety of long vowel sound-spellings, for example: widow, property, claim, agreed, continued, highest, solution, stake, closer, died. Tell students to make their best guess on spelling—placing the word in the correct column is the focus. After students have finished sorting the words, ask them to look for additional words with long vowel sound-spellings in paragraphs 1–2 of “The Dred Scott Decision” and add them to the chart.

Share

Invite groups to share their charts with the class. Ask students to discuss any challenges they had differentiating between vowel sounds or with spellings. Use this time to correct any spelling errors.

Reread to Apply Word Knowledge

Ask students to reread paragraph 1 of “The Dred Scott Decision” with a partner, paying attention to words with long vowel sounds. Ask partners to tell each other how understanding the many ways in which long vowel sounds can be spelled helps them read words correctly in the text. If necessary, model your thinking about one or two of the words.

Challenge Activity: Invite partners to do a blind sort, in which one partner calls out a word with one of the long vowel sound-spellings they learned and the partner identifies which long vowel sound it contains. Ask partners to take turns calling out five words each.

Spelling

Display the spelling words and read them aloud. Ask partners to take turns reading the list and pronouncing each word correctly, using what they know about long vowel sound-spellings. Then have partners collaborate to write each word in a sentence. Students should check each other’s spelling.
