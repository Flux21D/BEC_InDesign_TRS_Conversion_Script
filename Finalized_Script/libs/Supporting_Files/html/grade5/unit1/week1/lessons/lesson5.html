5 Analyze Genre Features: Informational Text (15–20 MIN.) RI.5.1, RI.5.3, R.I.5.5, W.5.10, SL.5.1a, SL.5.1b, SL.5.1c, SL.5.1d

Engage Thinking

Ask: What do you recall about the text we just read?

Remind students that “Creating the Constitution” was an informational text in which the author presented factual information about an historical event: the creation of the U.S. Constitution. Explain that this lesson will focus on understanding the features of an informational text that revolves around a social studies topic.

Introduce Genre

Display the Features of an Informational Text web, and discuss each genre feature. Use examples from “Creating the Constitution” to illustrate the features. Explain the elements of informational text:

  • An informational text is a nonfiction text that presents information in an accurate and organized way. It is often about a single subject such as an historical event, culture, or geography.
  • The main purpose of an informational text is to inform.
  • An informational text presents facts and the details that support them. The information may be supported by data, graphics, and expert evidence. The texts may include primary sources, such as photographs.

Features of an Informational Text Web

Productive Engagement: Peer Group

Distribute a two-column chart. Ask groups to skim and scan “Creating the Constitution” to find examples of each genre feature you introduced. Each group should designate a discussion director and a recorder to fill in the chart. Students should label the examples in the annotation margin of their text and be prepared to present their group’s findings.

Give students no more than seven to eight minutes. Monitor their conversations to identify students who struggle and students who are able to find examples in the text.

Share

Call on members of each group to share the group’s examples for one or more genre features. Encourage members of the other groups to build on ideas by presenting additional information. For example, while discussing Madison’s role in the Bill of Rights, ask students to share what they learned about the Bill of Rights. Model sentence frames that students can use to link their comments to those of the presenters.

  • [Student’s name]’s group found an example of the feature ___ in the text. We found another example of that feature. We found ___.
  • We found an example of a different genre feature. We found out about ___ .

If groups struggled with specific categories of genre features, model how you identified features using the sample chart provided.
