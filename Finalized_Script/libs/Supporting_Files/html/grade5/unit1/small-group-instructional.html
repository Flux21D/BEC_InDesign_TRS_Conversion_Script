Preteach/Reteach Unit Strategies and Skills

See the Additional Resource section for:

  • Routines to preteach and reteach unit reading and language standards.
  • Small-group texts by level for reteaching strategies and skills
Writing support in small groups: based on your observations, bring together students who need additional support to complete the writing tasks.

Independent and Collaborative Learning Time

Write Independently
Have individual students complete the independent writing tasks provided at the end of each day’s writing mini-lesson.

Answer Questions Using Text Evidence
Assign questions from the Text Evidence Question Card to individuals or partners based on the leveled texts they have read.

Read Independently
Make a range of informational and literary texts available for students to self select based on their interests. See the recommended trade books on Teacher Resource System page AR76 and Managing Your Independent Reading Program at www.BenchmarkUniverse.com.

Reinforce Word Study Skills and Spelling
Have individuals or partners complete the Word Study skills and Spelling activities for each week of instruction.

Connect Across Disciplines: Unit Projects
Deepen knowledge of California Science and History-Social Science concepts by assigning activities to groups or individuals to deepen their content knowledge. See the standards-based Unit 1 activities on pages AR2-AR3.

Build Fluency/Accelerate Reading Development
Week 1, Week 2:
Engage students in one or more of the following fluency-building activities:

  • Assign familiar reading to partners
  • Have students listen and read along to an e-book version of a previously read small-group text.

Week 3:
Have small groups rehearse their script to build fluency skills using Reader’s Theater or you may continue guided reading with leveled books.

Intervention

Use Intervention materials in tutorial or small-group settings.
These materials focus on students who need reteaching and practice in one or more of the four key foundational skills: print concepts, phonological awareness, phonics and word recognition, and fluency.

Assessment

Measure student progress and inform instruction through assessment opportunities.
Assessment opportunities include:

  • Weekly Assessments
  • Unit 1 Assessment
  • See pages 34, 36, and 98 for formative assessment opportunities in this unit.
