Read-Aloud and Book Discussion (25 Min.)

Smooth Transitions

If students sit at tables or in groups, you may want to call one table at a time to transition to the whole-group meeting area by using a signal, such as a hand bell, clapping, or flashing the lights. Remind students to push the chair in quietly and walk silently to the meeting area.

Getting Ready for Whole-Group Instruction: Annotations

Say: We have been learning and practicing numerous reading strategies that help us understand what we are reading. Today we will learn the strategy of annotation.

Show the students the following chart, which can be found in the inside front cover of Texts for Close Reading.

Symbol Purpose
Identify a key detail.
Star an important idea in the margin.
Mark a sequence of events.
Circle a key word or phrase.
Mark a question you have about information in the text. Write your question in the margin.
Indicate an idea in the text you find interesting. Comment on this idea in the margin.

After discussing each, share with the students that these notes are written along the margins of the text. If the text is not theirs, then they can use self-stick notes to do their annotating.

Say: I have a copy of a passage that I want to read to you. As I read, I will be thinking about what I am reading and how I will annotate this passage in order to help me understand it better. Watch as I read the first paragraph.

Read the paragraph aloud and make any related annotations in the margins, explaining as you go.

Ask: Are there any questions about what I just did? Now I will read the next paragraph. I want you to think of which annotations I should make in the margins.

You and the students will probably notice that there will be different suggestions for annotations, as this will vary according to each individual’s understanding of the passage.

Say: Tomorrow you will have a chance to practice this strategy with a partner.




Objectives

Students will:

  • Transition efficiently between mini-lessons.
  • Practice active listening.
  • Learn about annotating a text.

Additional Materials

  • Enlarged copy of a passage for annotation
  • Annotations chart (from the inside cover of Texts for Close Reading)
  • Self-Stick

Establishing Routines (5 Min.)

Establishing Workstation Routines

Have a Rotation Chart with student assignments ready to share with the class.

Say: Once again, we are going to practice having a few different workstations going at the same time. We will use this workstation chart to help us know what station we are supposed to be working at.

Explain to the students how to read the chart. Tell them who will be going where first. Remind students of the expectations for each workstation and call students to move to the appropriate station to get started. Go over the signal they need to listen for that will indicate they need to clean up their station and move to the next one. Remind the students that when they get to each station, they can read the activities chart to remind them of the choices they have once they are there.

As the students are working at the stations, you may walk around and monitor them as they work at the different stations. Answer any questions they may have, or redirect students that need additional support.

If you and the students feel comfortable with how the workstations are working, you may call a few students to come to your guided reading table and start working with small groups. You may also use this time for reading, writing, or word study assessments.

Repeat the rotational process three more times so that students have practiced getting started, cleaning up, and rotating to a new station four times.




Objectives

Students will:

  • Listen and respond to instructions.

Additional Materials

  • Rotation Chart

Independent Reading and Listening Workstations (30 Min.)

Independent Reading Workstation

The students will get to the station, select which reading activity they will work on, and continue working at this station until they hear the signal to switch.

Reading Workstation
Activities Expectations
  • independent reading (both silent and whisper reading)
  • buddy reading
  • previewing a new book
  • researching a topic of interest
  • completing a graphic organizer
  • work independently or with a buddy
  • use an inside voice when working with a buddy
  • clean up after yourself
  • help others keep the station neat and organized
  • be purposeful
  • be productive

Independent Listening Workstation

The students will get to the station, select which listening activity they will work on, and continue working at this station until they hear the signal to switch.

Listening Workstation
Activities Expectations
  • listen individually
  • listen with a buddy
  • work independently or with a buddy
  • use an inside voice when working with a buddy
  • troubleshoot equipment with a buddy before asking a teacher
  • clean up after yourself
  • help others keep the station neat and organized
  • be purposeful
  • be productive



Objectives

Students will:

  • Transition efficiently between workstations.

Additional Materials

  • Students’ independent reading of books
  • Reading logs
