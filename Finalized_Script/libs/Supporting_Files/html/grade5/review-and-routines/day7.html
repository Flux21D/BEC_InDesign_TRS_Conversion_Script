Establishing Routines (20 MIN.)

Smooth Transitions

If students sit at tables or in groups, you may want to call one table at a time to transition to the whole-group meeting area by using a signal, such as a hand bell, clapping, or flashing the lights. Remind students to push the chair in quietly and walk silently to the meeting area.

Getting Ready for Whole-Group Instruction: Activating Prior Knowledge

Choose a book that is meant to inform the reader, possibly one that is connected to a Social Studies or Science unit you are currently studying.

Say: Today, we are going to read the book (say name and author/illustrator). Take a look at the cover. What do you think this book is going to be about?

Say: Let’s take a look at the pictures, illustrations, and headings. What do the pictures and headings tell you about the book?

Ask: What do you think is the purpose of this book? Refer to the Author’s Purpose anchor chart if needed.

Ask: If our book is about ________, what types of words could we expect to hear?

Write down student answers on the board. Occasionally ask students to justify their word choice or expand on their thinking. You might use: Tell us more about your word. What makes you think your word might be in the book?

Say: Thinking about what vocabulary words we might find in a book is another strategy readers use before reading.




Objectives

Students will:

  • Transition efficiently between mini-lessons.
  • Practice active listening.

Additional Materials

  • Chart Paper
  • Markers
  • Selected book for read-aloud
  • Author's Puprose anchor chart

Read-Aloud and Book Discussion (30 MIN.)

Read-Aloud: Using a KWL Chart to Document Learning

Say: Today, each of you is going to complete your own KWL chart.

Pass out a chart to each student.

Say: Write the book topic in the first box. Go ahead and start listing things you think you already know about this topic. We have a great list of vocabulary words on the board that might help you as you write.

Book Topic
K
What I think I Know 		W
What I want to know 		L
What I learned









Say: Let's take a moment to share what we think we know in our table teams.

If desks are not already arranged in table teams, allow students time to form groups of three or four.

Say: Each of you is going to take turns sharing something from your chart. You will only share one statement at a time. When everyone in your group has had a turn, go around and share again. Pay attention to what others in the group say. Try to avoid repeating the same statement over and over again. When your group is finished sharing, take a moment to write down anything else you would like in the K section of your chart. I am sure you heard something that sparked some background knowledge for you.

Say: Let’s complete the W section of the chart before I begin reading. Take a few moments to jot down what you would like to know about this topic.

Model an example or two for the students before they begin filling in their own charts.

When students have completed the W section of their charts, begin the read-aloud. Pause and ask questions or clarify vocabulary words when appropriate. Let students know that they will have time to complete the L section of the chart after the read-aloud is finished.




Objectives

Student will:

  • Listen and respond to a story.

Additional Materials

  • Chart paper
  • Markers
  • Individual KWL chart for each student

Indepenedent Reading (20 MIN.)

Review the Reading Workstation

Say: We are going to start the Reading Workstation now. Think about what you plan to do with your time today.

Refer to the Reading Workstation chart as needed.

Reading Workstation
Activities Expectations
  • independent reading (both silent and whisper reading)
  • buddy reading
  • previewing a new book
  • researching a topic of interest
  • completing a graphic organizer
  • work independently or with a buddy
  • use an inside voice when working with a buddy
  • clean up after yourself
  • help others keep the station neat and organized
  • be purposeful
  • be productive

Say: When I give you the signal, please get settled quickly and quietly.

Give enough time at the end for students to complete their reading logs. Answer any questions students might have about the reading workstation.

Building the Writing Process (15 MIN.)

Constructive Feedback

Using the established signal, call students to the meeting area.

Say: Sometimes when we share our writing, it is important to be able to give and receive constructive criticism. The listener should make suggestions or offer advice to the writer. This gives the writer the opportunity to listen to feedback about his or her writing. Here are some ways you might choose to share your opinion:

  • I’m confused about _________.
  • Tell me more about _________.
  • Can you add ________?

Ask students to work with a partner and to give each other feedback on a piece of writing chosen from their process writing folders. If time allows, students may revise their writing based on comments from their partners.




Objectives

Students will:

  • Practice reading independently.

Additional Materials

  • Reading Workstation Chart
  • Students’ independent reading books
  • Reading logs
  • Response journals or writing folders

Independent Writing (15 MIN.)

Review the Writing Workstation

Have the Writing Workstation chart available for students to see.

Say: Yesterday we discussed what happens in the Writing Workstation. Take a moment to think about what we are expected to do in the Writing Workstation. What do you remember? What are things we might choose to do in this workstation? Turn and talk with a partner to discuss ideas.

Say: Let’s practice. Choose your writing activity for today and get started.

Writing Workstation
Activities Expectations
  • question in a jar
  • lift a line
  • opinion letters
  • share your writing
  • writing to sources
  • begin a new piece of writing
  • work independently or with a buddy
  • use an inside voice when working with a buddy
  • practice respectful conversations
  • clean up after yourself
  • help others keep the station neat and organized
  • be purposeful
  • be productive

Give enough time at the end for students to clean up. Answer any questions students might have about the Writing Workstation.




Objectives

Student will:

  • Practice working at the Writing Workstation.
